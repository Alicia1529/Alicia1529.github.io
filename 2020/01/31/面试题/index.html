  1. Describe a time when you took a creative approach to solving a research problem.

In the summer of my junior year, I conducted a research on developing a mobile restaurant recommendation engine for the elderly people with mobility constraints. A major complication for this research was that the candidate restaurants will change as users change their location and we could not get enough training data to characterize all restaurants in New York City. What is worse, we could not connect to the live Yelp database, but can only use their constrained API service.
Therefore, I decided to use the contextual bandit algorithm to model the external features of the restaurants instead of characterizing each restaurant with vectors. Another problem was lack of historical data. In order to solve this probelm, I determined to have users generate feedback and collect it in real-time. Then the recommendation model will be updated according to usersâ€™ feedback and new recommendations will be made accordingly. This restaurant recommendation service ended up receiving very positive reviews from the elderly during the beta test and can support up to 1,000 users.
